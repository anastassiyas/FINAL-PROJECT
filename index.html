The Republic of Kazakhstan

    Introduction

The Republic of Kazakhstan is situated at the heart of Eurasia and is the worlds 9th largest country. When Kazakhstan became independent in the unstable days of December 1991, few people thought the country would achieve as much as it did in its first 20 years. Instead of disintegrating, Kazakhstan’s economy became one of the fastest growing in the world, and the country has built a robust political system of presidential and parliamentary democracy with its media and NGOs enjoying a growing voice and role in shaping the society.

    Location

  • The area of the territory — 2 724 902 km2.
  • The population is about 19 million people.
  • It borders Russia to the North and West, China to the East, and Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan to the South.
  • The Capital is Nur-Sultan.
  • The largest city with a population of more than 1.8 million people is Almaty.
  • It occupies the 9th place in the world by territory.

      People of Kazakhstan

  • Many nationalities live together in Kazakhstan. Kazakhstan's 131 ethnic groups included Kazakhs (63 % of the population), Russians, Uzbeks, Ukrainians, Germans, Tatars, Uighurs and many others.
  • Islam is the religion of about 70 % of the population, Christianity-26 %. Kazakhstan officially declares religious freedom, but religious leaders who oppose the government are suppressed.

      Politics of Kazakhstan

  • Kazakhstan is a presidential Republic
  • Kazakhstan has a bicameral Parliament consisting of the Mazhilis (lower house) and the Senate (upper house).
  • Three parties are officially represented in Kazakhstan:
  • Nur Otan party, the country's ruling party
  • AK Zhol democratic party of Kazakhstan, a parliamentary party
  • Communist people's party of Kazakhstan, parliamentary party.
  • All other parties in Kazakhstan are prohibited.
